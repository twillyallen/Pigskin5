NFL Trade Rumors 2026: Blockbuster Deals That Could Reshape the League

By Twillyallen | March 2026 | 9 min read

The NFL trade market has become more active in recent years than at any point in league history. What once felt like rare, dramatic events — a franchise quarterback changing teams, a star receiver forcing his way out, a defensive cornerstone getting moved for draft capital — now happen with enough regularity that fans barely have time to process one deal before the next one drops. The 2026 offseason appears poised to continue that trend, with several high-profile players whose situations carry legitimate trade rumors.

Here's a look at the trade scenarios worth monitoring and the teams most likely to be players in blockbuster deals this offseason.

Why Trades Have Increased

Understanding the rise in NFL trades requires understanding the economics driving them. The salary cap has grown substantially, but so have player salaries — particularly at premium positions like quarterback, edge rusher, and wide receiver. Teams that built around a player when he was on his rookie contract often find themselves in an impossible financial situation when that contract expires. Extending the player costs so much cap space that it cripples roster depth elsewhere. Moving the player for draft capital resets the roster but creates a competitive gap.

This tension is playing out across the league simultaneously, which is why so many teams find themselves at trade inflection points at the same time. Add in the increasing player empowerment movement — where stars leverage no-trade clauses, restructuring demands, and public pressure to control their destinations — and the result is a trade market more dynamic than anything the NFL has seen before.

Teams Most Likely to Sell

Teams entering legitimate rebuilds have the clearest incentive to trade veteran stars for draft picks. A team that finishes the season with a losing record, recognizes it has a multi-year rebuild ahead, and holds veterans who won't be part of that rebuilt roster has every reason to convert those players into future assets. The challenge is finding trade partners willing to pay the asking price — and determining what that price should be.

The calculus for selling teams involves evaluating not just what a player is worth today, but what he'll be worth in year two and three of a new contract. Stars coming off peak seasons command premium prices because buyers are paying for what just happened as much as what's projected. Sellers who move players just before production begins declining maximize their return. It's timing that's easier to describe than execute.

Teams Most Likely to Buy

On the other side, teams that feel they're one or two players away from championship contention are the most motivated trade buyers. These are franchises with strong quarterback play, solid coaching, and rosters that are good but not yet great. Adding a proven star through trade — even at the cost of significant draft capital — can accelerate their window in ways that the draft alone cannot match.

The risk for buying teams is overpaying and mortgaging draft capital that proves impossible to replace. History is littered with examples of teams that traded multiple first-round picks for a player who didn't deliver the championship they were chasing. The Washington Commanders trading for Trent Williams's prime years. The San Francisco 49ers moving up for defensive talent. These decisions looked reasonable at the time and produced mixed results at best. Any team considering a major trade in 2026 should study those precedents carefully.

Quarterback Trades: The Highest Stakes

No trade carries higher stakes — or higher price tags — than a quarterback swap. The NFL's most dramatic recent offseasons have been defined by quarterback movement: Russell Wilson to Denver, Matthew Stafford to Los Angeles, Deshaun Watson to Cleveland. These deals reshape franchises, generate enormous media coverage, and set off chain reactions across the league as teams adjust to the new competitive landscape.

The 2026 offseason has at least one significant quarterback situation worth monitoring closely. Teams that finished last season without a clear answer at the position will be aggressive in pursuing any available option, which drives up prices for teams willing to trade a starter. Expect at least one significant quarterback deal to either be completed or heavily rumored before the draft.

Draft Pick Value in Modern Trades

One fascinating evolution in the NFL trade market is the changing value placed on draft picks. Teams have become more analytically sophisticated in understanding what each draft slot is worth, which has created a more efficient market for trades involving picks. It's harder to steal a deal than it used to be, because both sides have access to similar information about pick value.

What remains inefficient is the evaluation of players themselves. One team's assessment of a player's value can differ dramatically from another's, creating genuine opportunities for deals that benefit both sides. The teams with the best scouting and evaluation infrastructure tend to win trades over time — not because they're luckier, but because their information is more reliable than their counterparts.

Whatever happens in the 2026 trade market, one thing is certain: the offseason will reshape the competitive landscape in ways that won't be fully understood until the season actually begins.
