Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 28, 2024

Introduction

Pigskin 5 ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website pigskin5.com.

Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access the site.

Information We Collect

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit Pigskin 5, certain information about your device and usage may be automatically collected, including:

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • IP address
  • Time and date of visit
  • Pages viewed and time spent on pages
  • Referring website addresses

Local Storage

Pigskin 5 uses your browser's local storage to save your game data locally on your device. This includes:

  • Daily quiz completion status
  • Your daily streak count
  • Your touchdown streak count
  • Previous quiz scores and results
  • Leaderboard submissions (username and scores only)

This data is stored only on your device and is not transmitted to our servers unless you choose to submit your score to the leaderboard. You can clear this data at any time through your browser settings.

Leaderboard Information

If you choose to submit your score to the daily leaderboard, we collect:

  • Your chosen display name
  • Your quiz score
  • Your average completion time

We do not collect email addresses or other personal identifying information for leaderboard submissions.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Operate and maintain the Pigskin 5 game
  • Display daily leaderboards
  • Improve user experience and game functionality
  • Analyze site usage and trends
  • Display relevant advertisements
  • Prevent fraudulent activity and abuse

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device to store data that can be recalled by a web server. We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to access or store information.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences or submitting your score to the leaderboard.

Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics services to help us understand how visitors interact with our website. These cookies collect information about how you use the site, such as which pages you visit most often.

Advertising Cookies

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on Pigskin 5. Google and its partners use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our site and other sites on the internet.

Third-Party Advertising

Google AdSense

We use Google AdSense to monetize our website. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visit to Pigskin 5 and/or other sites on the Internet.

Google may use the DoubleClick cookie, which enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visits to this site and other sites on the Internet. The DoubleClick cookie is used by Google in the ads served on the websites of its partners, such as websites displaying AdSense ads or participating in Google certified ad networks.

When users visit a partner's website, a cookie may be dropped on that end user's browser. Third party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on someone's past visits to Pigskin 5.

Opting Out of Personalized Advertising

You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting:

  • Google Ads Settings
  • Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out page
  • Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of this and many other websites you visit.

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (such as our Twitter/X account). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third-party sites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Tracking is not the same as using or collecting information in connection with a website. For these purposes, tracking refers to collecting personally identifiable information from consumers who use or visit a website as they move across different websites over time. Our website does not track its visitors over time and across third-party websites. However, some third-party sites may keep track of your browsing activities when they serve you content, which enables them to tailor what they present to you.
